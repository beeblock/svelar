# OKRs and KPIs Guide

Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) align teams on ambitious goals and measure progress with quantifiable outcomes.

## What are OKRs?

**Objective**: Qualitative, aspirational goal (the "what")
**Key Results**: Quantitative, measurable outcomes (the "how we know")

### Format

```
Objective: [Inspiring, qualitative goal]
├── Key Result 1: [Measurable outcome with target]
├── Key Result 2: [Measurable outcome with target]
└── Key Result 3: [Measurable outcome with target]
```

### Good Example

**Objective**: Become the preferred platform for small business e-commerce

**Key Results**:
1. Increase monthly active merchants from 1,000 to 5,000
2. Achieve NPS of 50+ (currently 35)
3. Grow monthly GMV from $1M to $5M
4. Reduce time-to-first-sale from 7 days to 2 days

### Bad Example

**Objective**: Build more features ❌ (Output, not outcome)

**Key Results**:
1. Ship 10 new features ❌ (Activity, not impact)
2. Hold 20 user interviews ❌ (Activity, not result)
3. Increase conversion rate ❌ (No target or baseline)

## OKR Best Practices

### Objectives

**Characteristics**:
- Inspiring and memorable
- Qualitative (not a number)
- Time-bound (quarterly or annual)
- Ambitious but achievable
- Aligned with company strategy

**Good Objectives**:
- "Delight customers with world-class support"
- "Become the fastest way to deploy applications"
- "Make financial planning accessible to everyone"

**Bad Objectives**:
- "Increase revenue by 20%" (This is a key result)
- "Ship the mobile app" (This is an output)
- "Improve the product" (Too vague)

### Key Results

**Characteristics**:
- Measurable with a number
- Specific target and baseline
- Outcome-focused, not output
- 3-5 per objective (not more)
- Challenging but achievable (70% success is good)

**Good Key Results**:
- "Increase trial-to-paid conversion from 15% to 25%"
- "Reduce average support response time from 4 hours to 1 hour"
- "Achieve 90% customer satisfaction score (currently 75%)"

**Bad Key Results**:
- "Launch new features" (Output, not outcome)
- "Improve performance" (Not measurable)
- "Increase user engagement" (No target)

## OKR Levels

### Company OKRs (Annual)

Strategic, high-level goals for the entire organization.

**Example**:
**Objective**: Establish market leadership in SMB analytics

**Key Results**:
1. Reach $50M ARR (currently $20M)
2. Acquire 10,000 customers (currently 3,000)
3. Achieve #1 ranking in G2 category
4. Expand to 5 new markets

### Department OKRs (Quarterly)

How each department contributes to company OKRs.

**Product Team Example**:
**Objective**: Accelerate product adoption and retention

**Key Results**:
1. Increase 30-day retention from 35% to 50%
2. Reduce time-to-value from 14 days to 5 days
3. Achieve 80% feature adoption for core workflows
4. Improve NPS from 40 to 55

### Individual OKRs (Quarterly)

How each person contributes to department OKRs.

**Product Manager Example**:
**Objective**: Drive user activation and engagement

**Key Results**:
1. Launch improved onboarding flow increasing activation by 20%
2. Ship 3 features that move core engagement metrics
3. Reduce drop-off in signup flow from 50% to 30%
4. Achieve 90% sprint goal completion rate

## Common Metrics and KPIs

### Acquisition Metrics

- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: Cost to acquire one customer
- **Conversion Rate**: % of visitors who become customers
- **Signup Rate**: % of visitors who create account
- **Traffic Sources**: Organic, paid, referral, direct
- **Landing Page Conversion**: % who convert from landing page

### Activation Metrics

- **Time to Value**: How long until user gets value
- **Activation Rate**: % of signups who complete key action
- **Onboarding Completion**: % who finish onboarding flow
- **First Action**: % who complete critical first action
- **Setup Completion**: % who fully configure product

### Engagement Metrics

- **DAU (Daily Active Users)**: Users active each day
- **MAU (Monthly Active Users)**: Users active each month
- **DAU/MAU Ratio**: Stickiness metric (>20% is good)
- **Session Frequency**: How often users return
- **Session Duration**: How long users stay
- **Feature Adoption**: % using specific features
- **Core Action Completion**: % completing key workflows

### Retention Metrics

- **Retention Rate**: % of users who return
  - Day 1, Day 7, Day 30, Month 3
- **Churn Rate**: % of users who leave
- **Cohort Retention**: Retention by signup cohort
- **Resurrection Rate**: % of churned users who return

### Revenue Metrics

- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**: Predictable monthly revenue
- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**: MRR × 12
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**: Revenue / # of users
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: Total revenue from average customer
- **LTV:CAC Ratio**: Should be >3:1
- **Expansion Revenue**: Upsells and cross-sells

### Product Metrics

- **Feature Usage**: % of users using feature
- **Error Rate**: % of requests that fail
- **Load Time**: Page/API response times
- **Crash Rate**: % of sessions with crashes
- **Bug Count**: Open bugs by severity

### Customer Satisfaction

- **NPS (Net Promoter Score)**: Likelihood to recommend (-100 to 100)
- **CSAT (Customer Satisfaction)**: Satisfaction rating (1-5)
- **CES (Customer Effort Score)**: Ease of use (1-7)
- **Support Tickets**: Volume and resolution time
- **Reviews/Ratings**: App store or review site ratings

## North Star Metric

The single metric that best captures the core value delivered.

### Examples by Industry

**E-commerce**: Purchases per month
**Social Media**: Daily active users
**SaaS**: Weekly active users completing core action
**Marketplace**: Gross merchandise value (GMV)
**Streaming**: Hours watched per week
**Productivity**: Documents created per week

### Choosing Your North Star

**Criteria**:
1. Captures value to customers
2. Reflects business success
3. Actionable by team
4. Measurable and trackable
5. Leading indicator of growth

**Example**: Spotify
- **North Star**: Time spent listening
- **Why**: More listening = more engagement = more retention = more revenue
- **Not**: Song plays (can be gamed), Sign-ups (vanity metric)

## Pirate Metrics (AARRR)

Framework for product growth metrics.

### Acquisition

How do users find you?
- Traffic sources
- Conversion rates by source
- CAC by channel
- Brand awareness

### Activation

Do they have a great first experience?
- Signup completion rate
- Time to "aha moment"
- Onboarding completion
- First key action taken

### Retention

Do they come back?
- Day 1, 7, 30 retention
- Cohort retention curves
- Churn rate
- Reasons for churn

### Revenue

Do they pay?
- Conversion to paid
- ARPU
- MRR growth
- Payment success rate

### Referral

Do they tell others?
- Referral rate
- Viral coefficient (k-factor)
- NPS
- Social shares

## Setting Effective OKRs

### Step 1: Start with Strategy

Align with company vision and strategy.

**Question**: What must we achieve this quarter to move toward our vision?

### Step 2: Draft Objectives

Brainstorm aspirational goals.

**Tips**:
- Focus on outcomes, not outputs
- Make them inspiring
- 3-5 objectives maximum
- Clear owner for each

### Step 3: Define Key Results

For each objective, define 3-5 measurable outcomes.

**Template**:
- Increase [metric] from [X] to [Y]
- Achieve [metric] of [target]
- Launch [deliverable] resulting in [metric change]
- Reduce [metric] from [X] to [Y]

### Step 4: Validate and Align

Review with stakeholders:
- Do these ladder up to company OKRs?
- Are they achievable but ambitious?
- Can we influence these metrics?
- Do we have baseline data?

### Step 5: Commit and Communicate

Finalize and share broadly.

## OKR Scoring

### Grading Scale

- **0.0-0.3**: Red (Significant issues)
- **0.4-0.6**: Yellow (Made progress but fell short)
- **0.7-1.0**: Green (Success!)

**Note**: 70% achievement is good. OKRs should be ambitious.

### Scoring Methods

**Binary (Did we achieve it?)**:
- 0 = No
- 1 = Yes

**Graded (How much progress?)**:
- 0.0 = No progress
- 0.3 = Some progress
- 0.5 = Halfway there
- 0.7 = Mostly achieved
- 1.0 = Fully achieved

**Example**:
**KR**: Increase MRR from $100K to $200K
- Achieved: $170K
- Score: 0.7 (70% of $100K increase)

## OKR Tracking

### Weekly Check-ins

Quick status updates:
- Current progress on each KR
- On track (green), at risk (yellow), off track (red)
- Blockers and help needed
- Actions for next week

### Monthly Reviews

Deeper review:
- Progress toward each KR
- Adjust tactics if needed
- Identify risks
- Celebrate wins

### Quarterly Retrospective

End-of-quarter review:
- Final scores for each OKR
- What worked / what didn't
- Lessons learned
- Input for next quarter's OKRs

## Common OKR Mistakes

### 1. Confusing Outputs with Outcomes

**Bad**: "Ship mobile app" (output)
**Good**: "Increase mobile user engagement by 30%" (outcome)

### 2. Too Many OKRs

**Problem**: Diluted focus, nothing gets done
**Solution**: 3-5 objectives maximum, 3-5 KRs each

### 3. Not Measurable

**Bad**: "Improve user experience"
**Good**: "Increase NPS from 40 to 55"

### 4. Sandbagging (Too Easy)

**Problem**: Goals are guaranteed wins, no stretch
**Solution**: Set ambitious goals where 70% is success

### 5. Not Aligning

**Problem**: Team OKRs don't ladder up to company OKRs
**Solution**: Explicit alignment in OKR docs

### 6. Set and Forget

**Problem**: Define once, never review
**Solution**: Weekly check-ins, monthly reviews

## Real-World Example: SaaS Product

### Company OKR (Annual 2024)

**Objective**: Become the leading analytics platform for SMBs

**Key Results**:
1. Reach $10M ARR (currently $3M)
2. Acquire 2,000 customers (currently 500)
3. Achieve #1 G2 ranking in category
4. Expand to 3 new markets (UK, Canada, Australia)

### Product Team OKRs (Q2 2024)

**Objective 1**: Accelerate user activation and retention

**Key Results**:
1. Increase trial-to-paid conversion from 15% to 25%
2. Improve 30-day retention from 40% to 55%
3. Reduce time-to-first-insight from 7 days to 2 days
4. Achieve 80% activation rate for new signups

**Objective 2**: Drive product-led growth

**Key Results**:
1. Launch referral program generating 20% of new signups
2. Increase viral coefficient from 0.2 to 0.5
3. Achieve 100 organic reviews with 4.5+ rating
4. Grow self-serve revenue from 40% to 60% of total

**Objective 3**: Establish enterprise credibility

**Key Results**:
1. Launch SSO and advanced permissions
2. Achieve SOC 2 compliance certification
3. Close 10 enterprise deals ($50K+ ARR each)
4. Reduce enterprise sales cycle from 90 to 45 days

### PM Individual OKRs (Q2 2024)

**Objective**: Ship features that drive activation and retention

**Key Results**:
1. Launch improved onboarding reducing time-to-value by 50%
2. Ship 3 high-impact features increasing engagement by 25%
3. Achieve 90% sprint goal completion rate
4. Reduce product-related support tickets by 30%

### Tracking Dashboard

|-----------|--------|---------|-------|--------|
| Trial-to-paid conversion | 25% | 20% | 0.5 | 🟡 Yellow |
| 30-day retention | 55% | 48% | 0.5 | 🟡 Yellow |

## Summary

**Effective OKRs**:
1. Ambitious but achievable (70% success is good)
2. Focus on outcomes, not outputs
3. Measurable with clear targets
4. Aligned with company strategy
5. Tracked regularly and adjusted as needed
6. 3-5 objectives, 3-5 key results each
7. Inspiring and memorable objectives
8. Quantifiable and time-bound key results

**Remember**: OKRs are a tool for alignment and focus, not a performance review system. They should inspire teams to achieve ambitious goals while maintaining flexibility to learn and adapt.
